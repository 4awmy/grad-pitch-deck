AI Immune System

Computer vision for haematology — because manual WBC counting is too slow.

YOLOv8 Python FastAPI OpenCV PyTorch

The Problem

Manual White Blood Cell (WBC) differential counting under microscopes is slow, tedious, and prone to subjective human error. In regions outside major cities in Egypt, access to expert hematologists is severely restricted, delaying critical diagnoses like leukemia, infections, and anemia.

What We Build

A web-based computer vision assistant. Lab technicians upload digitized blood smear microscope slide images. The backend uses YOLOv8 object detection to locate, classify, and count all primary WBC types in seconds, outputting a structured differential counting report.

How It Works

01

Slide Upload

A lab technician captures slide images using an optical microscope camera mount and uploads the file to the React portal.

02

Cell Detection & Localization

Our fine-tuned YOLOv8 model runs inference to locate individual leukocytes, ignoring red blood cells and noise artifacts.

03

Classification & Categorization

Cells are cataloged into five major categories: neutrophils, lymphocytes, monocytes, eosinophils, and basophils.

04

Report Compilation

Counts are converted into standard relative percentages, compiling a clean pathology report in digital PDF format.

Technology Mapping

YOLOv8
Provides concurrent localization and classification of cells in high-resolution images.
FastAPI
Enables fast binary streaming pipelines to send microscope image bytes to backend networks.
OpenCV
Handles preprocessing, slide thresholding, and cropping operations.

Data Strategy

LISC Dataset
Standard open dataset containing annotated leukocytes for model pre-training.
CellaVision Benchmark
Using verified clinical images to benchmark cell detection accuracy and F1 scores.
Hospital Partnership
Planned partnership with local Cairo clinical labs to gather local sample types.

Why This is Publishable

We present a comparative analysis of cell-detection models (YOLO variants vs. CNN architectures) on low-power hardware. We publish local hospital test data metrics validated by professional pathologists.

Target Publication Venues

Targeting Elsevier Computers in Biology and Medicine or IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics (JBHI).
