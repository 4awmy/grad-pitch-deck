Idea 02 - Accessibility Hardware

Sign Language Glove

A wearable prototype that reads hand gestures through embedded sensors and translates signs into text or speech for real-time communication.

The problem

Many hearing and speech-impaired people need a human interpreter for everyday interactions. That creates barriers in clinics, schools, offices, and public services.

What we build

A glove prototype that captures finger bend and hand orientation, classifies gestures, and outputs the meaning on a connected screen or speaker.

How it works

Flex sensors and an inertial unit stream signals to a microcontroller. A compact classifier maps signal patterns to a trained gesture vocabulary.

Research angle

The paper can compare sensor combinations, classification accuracy, real-time latency, and the feasibility of collecting a local Arabic sign dataset.

Social impact

The project can become a low-cost accessibility tool and a dataset contribution for Egyptian Arabic sign language research.
