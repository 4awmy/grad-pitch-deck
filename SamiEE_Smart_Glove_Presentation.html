Slide 1: The Problem
The Communication Barrier & Technical Bottleneck
The Current State
Today, deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals in Egypt rely on manual human interpreters, slow written notes, or basic generic translation apps that fail to recognize Egyptian Sign Language (ArSL) or local dialect nuances.
Technical Friction
Existing vision-based tools suffer from high latency, sensitivity to lighting, camera occlusions, and severe computational bottlenecks when tracking dynamic 3D hand trajectories in real time.
🔀 The Core Dilemma
Users are currently forced to choose between Option A: slow, inconvenient manual human communication and Option B: expensive, non-localized enterprise systems that lack real-time spoken Egyptian Arabic.
📉 The Bottleneck: Camera Vision vs. Wearable Telemetry
RGB Camera setups drop to <40% accuracy in low-light environments or when fingers self-occlude during rapid signing. A wearable hardware solution eliminates line-of-sight dependencies entirely through direct kinematic sensing.

Slide 2: What We Build
Sami'ee (سميع): Real-Time Sign Translation Glove

A self-contained wearable hardware system fitted with precision flex sensors and 9-axis IMUs designed to automate and optimize sign language translation into spoken and written Egyptian Arabic.

1. Automated Sensing
Automatically captures high-frequency 10-axis finger bending angles and 3D hand orientation/acceleration at 100Hz without requiring external cameras or manual data alignment.
2. Intelligent NLP Engine
Applies lightweight machine learning (1D-CNN / Bi-LSTM hybrid neural models) to recognize dynamic sign trajectories and map them directly to Egyptian Arabic vocabulary and idioms.
🔊 3. Actionable Execution
Delivers instantaneous output via an OLED wrist display and an integrated audio synthesizer (or BLE mobile app) speaking natural Egyptian Arabic text-to-speech (TTS) in real time.

Slide 3: How It Works
System Architecture & Spatial-Temporal Pipeline

1. Input Layer

5x Resistive Flex Sensors & MPU-6050/9250 9-axis IMU capture raw kinematic voltage signals.

2. Preprocessing

ESP32/STM32 MCU applies Kalman filtering and moving-average noise reduction at 100Hz.

3. Core Engine

Quantized Bi-LSTM / 1D-CNN neural network classifies static and dynamic sign trajectories.

4. Output & UI

Egyptian Arabic Lexicon mapping pushes spoken TTS audio and OLED/mobile text display.

🎛 Hardware & Preprocessing
Analog voltages from flex sensors and raw gyroscope/accelerometer vectors are sampled by an ESP32 microcontroller. Embedded digital signal processing eliminates drift and environmental tremor noise.
Neural Inference & Lexicon
Our hybrid architecture captures both local finger patterns (CNN) and sequential motion over time (LSTM), matching features against an SQLite/JSON Egyptian Arabic grammatical lexicon.

Slide 4: Research Angle
Academic Contribution & Empirical Benchmarking

The Thesis Question: How does our multi-sensor embedded gesture classification approach compare against traditional computer-vision baselines under constrained real-world conditions (variable lighting, motion artifacts, battery constraints)?

Execution Speed & Latency
Measuring sensor sampling, neural inference time, and TTS generation delay across varying vocabulary sizes. Target: <150ms total end-to-end latency.
🎯 Accuracy & Error Rate (WER)
Evaluating classification precision, recall, and F1-score across 50+ core Egyptian Arabic everyday signs compared to standard RGB-camera baseline models.
🔋 Resource & Energy Efficiency
Tracking microcontroller memory footprint (SRAM/Flash usage), CPU clock utilization, and battery consumption (mA/hour) during continuous real-time signing.
📈 Vocabulary Scalability
Benchmarking classification accuracy degradation and inference time scaling as the sign library expands from a 20-word baseline to 200+ localized phrases.
